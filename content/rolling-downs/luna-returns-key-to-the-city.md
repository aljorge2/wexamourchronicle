I received the Key to the City when the Office of Resource and Civil Management climbed up my sandbag pile at the Millhaven Grange Hall. They said some kind words, and then went on their merry way after he told the Chronicle it was the first time the honour had gone to someone who runs a relief yard from the top of the bags. I am giving the key back.

I want to be plain about why, because the people who gave it to me will want to make it about modesty, and it is not about that. I ran that sandbag line for the whole of the wet season. I know exactly what came up the Downs road from Vaelthir during those weeks, because I stood at the top of the pile and watched the road. The river crested at Brannock Crossing, the southern basin went over at three points, the south lane washed out, and the capital that is so quick with a ceremony instead of any relief. The fairies from the tinker's workshop did more for this region in an afternoon than Vaelthir has done all spring, and they did it for nothing and asked for nothing back.

And it is not only the Downs that is suffering. Ask the Greymane families. The Market Hall in the Lower Trades District burned, and those people are still in grange housing while the office that minds the city's coin finds time to award a key and not time to rebuild its own city. Vaelthir is very good at pomp and circumstance. It is no good at all at the part that requires actual investment and caring. I have now seen both parts up close, and I will not stand on the pile and accept the first while the second goes undone.

I have heard the old story out here, the one about the fires before the Silence and the families on the far shore who lost everything and waited on help from the capital. People say it made at least one of them hard, and bitter, and finally gone from the Downs for good. I used to think that was a sad story about one woman. I do not think that anymore. I think it is a continuing tale I have been standing in all season, told again with my own neighbors in it, and I think the capital learned nothing from the first woman and hopes that we do not realize we're still in it.

So here is the key. The man from the office may come up the pile and collect it, or I will leave it at the Grange Hall with the donated gloves, where it will be of about as much use. When Vaelthir sends a road crew to the south lane, when the Greymane families have a market hall and a roof, when the relief that was promised actually comes up the Downs road, the city can keep its key and I will run the bags for nothing, the way I have all along. Until then I do not want a thing that opens a city that will not open its door to us.

*Luna ran the sandbag distribution and relief yard at the Millhaven Grange Hall through the Fenwater flood. She can be found most afternoons on the warm stones by the kitchen door, and reached by owl, though she rarely answers.*

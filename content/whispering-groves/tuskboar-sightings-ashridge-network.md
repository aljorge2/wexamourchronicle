I have been saying for two seasons that the tuskboars on the Ashridge network are not behaving the way they used to, and after this spring I am done hedging about it. The northeast Groves have always had boars, but they kept to the north above the upper junction and mostly wanted nothing to do with us. That has changed.

Sightings on the lower Ashridge trails are up sharply, the animals people are describing are noticeably larger than the ones I grew up giving a wide berth, and they are no longer turning around at the tree line. The above average rainfall pushed the forage closer to settled country and the boars followed it, which means they are now rooting through ground that runs right up against the townline, including the wooded slope directly above the Grove hospital. That last part is the part that worries everyone.

A tuskboar is not malicious, but it is half a ton of bad eyesight and worse temper, and a hospital is precisely the wrong place to have one come crashing through a hedge at dusk. The Groves Council knows. Whether the Council does anything before someone gets hurt is a separate question, and if their record on the DellRiven trail maintenance is any guide, I would not hold your breath. Until then: make noise on the lower Ashridge trails, keep dogs leashed and children close, do not get between a boar and whatever it is eating, and if you are visiting the hospital after dark, take the main road and not the canopy path. I will keep reporting as I hear more.

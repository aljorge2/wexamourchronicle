Posting this so the rest of the lane knows. Sometime in the night between market day and the one after, a quantity of goods went out of my back cellar: two wheels of the hard cheese, the better part of a barrel of aged cider, a string of dried river fowl, and my good copper pot, which is the part I am actually sore about. I know who did it and so do most of you. It was the serpent-pirate woman they call Rain. She came to my door two days prior asking after the road north, perfectly civil, drank a cup of my cider on the step and complimented it, and I understand now she was taking inventory. I am not asking anyone to go after her, and I want to be clear about that, because she is bigger than all of us, faster than she looks, and from what the dock men say she has done this up and down the coast and never once been caught at it. There is no reward on this notice, as a reward would only send some fool up to the Downs looking for her ship off the coast. All I am asking is that you mind your stores and if a polite stranger in a red coat admires your preserves, give her directions and nothing else, for she does not take from the same house twice, which is the only orderly thing about her. And if she happens to read this, which I doubt, the cheese and the cider are fairly hers now, but I would like the pot back.

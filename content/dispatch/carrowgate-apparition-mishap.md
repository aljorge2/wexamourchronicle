<strong style="color: var(--gold); display: block;">Twelfth hour</strong>

The Carrowgate point has reopened and normal crossings have resumed. The prosecutor's office has stated that Raz Dro'shaveen will be fined for his disruption of the transit point, but that the matter is not a criminal case. This concludes our live coverage.

<strong style="color: var(--gold); display: block;">Eleventh and a half hour</strong>

Officials have confirmed the cause of the accident. The apparition failure occurred when Dro'shaveen attempted to cross while juggling eleven live eels, eating a bowl of stew, and reading aloud from a book he described as "very engrossing." Witnesses confirm he had been advised four times not to do this.

<strong style="color: var(--gold); display: block;">Eleventh hour</strong>

Officials have named the individual under investigation as Raz Dro'shaveen, a traveling merchant. No charges have been filed at this time.

<strong style="color: var(--gold); display: block;">Tenth and a quarter hour</strong>

The one reported injury has been confirmed. Officials say the injured person is being investigated as the individual responsible for the apparition failure.

<strong style="color: var(--gold); display: block;">Ninth and three-quarter hour</strong>

A senior official has now arrived at the Carrowgate site and met briefly with crews behind the cordon. Following the meeting, the official told the Chronicle that the response is "proceeding as expected" and that "the meeting was productive and the public will be informed soon."

<strong style="color: var(--gold); display: block;">Eighth and a half hour</strong>

Officials on the scene are investigating this as a possible Apparition Failure.

<strong style="color: var(--gold); display: block;">Eighth hour</strong>

An incident at the eastern crossing near Vaelthir University, Carrowgate Transit Point. Witnesses described a sound "like a crack followed by white flashes". There is at least one reported injury. The Carrowgate point has been closed until further notice, and queued travelers were turned away. Officials on scene declined to comment. Crossings remain available at the Northbank, Westeros, and Southling points, though wait times are reported to exceed two hours.

The Chronicle is reporting live from the scene and will update as details become available.

**ROLLING DOWNS** — The Office of Resource and Civil Management has issued a flood advisory for the Rolling Downs region, effective immediately, following reports that the Fenwater River has exceeded its safe threshold at three monitored points along the southern basin.

Sustained runoff from the Greypeak range, where snowmelt has continued uninterrupted since the onset of the Silence, has pushed water levels to their highest recorded mark this cycle. ORCM hydrological officers confirmed that the river crested overnight at Brannock Crossing and is expected to remain elevated through the week.

Travel advisories are in effect for the following routes:

- The Lower Fenwater Road between Millhaven and Brannock Crossing is closed to cart and carriage traffic until further notice.
- The Greystone Bridge remains open but is subject to inspection. Travelers should expect delays.
- Ferry service at Wending Dock has been suspended.

Residents in low-lying areas along the southern Fenwater basin are advised to secure livestock, elevate stored goods, and prepare for possible evacuation if conditions worsen. Sandbag distribution points have been established at the Millhaven Grange Hall and the Brannock Crossing waystation.

ORCM officials noted that this is the fourth advisory of its kind in the past two years. "The melt does not stop," a regional coordinator told the Chronicle. "We plan for it, but the river does not care about plans."

The advisory will be reviewed in 48 hours. Updates will follow as conditions change.

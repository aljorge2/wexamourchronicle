I have listened to the Fenmore's Taco Club sponsor read on the Vaelthir Hour, by my own honest count, upward of forty times. Where every filling is fire-forged and every shell is spell-crisped. It plays between the news and the weather and it has lodged itself in my head the way only a good jingle and a bad one both can. Readers have written to ask whether the food lives up to the poetry, and a critic who reviews only the places that did not pay for the advertisement is not a critic but a coward. So I walked out to 5060 Newport Avenue, near the Nascium Playhouse, open dawn to dusk, and I ordered the entire signature menu. Here is where each one lands, worst to first, because you came for the ranking and I am not going to make you wait for it.

## Third place, and it is not close: The Anomaly

At ten gold it is the most expensive thing on the board, and Fenmore's clearly wants you to believe it is the crown of the menu. Aetheric-glazed roast, gold cheese, lich-pepper sauce. I want to be fair to it. The roast underneath all of that is a genuinely good piece of meat, slow and dark and yielding. But nobody at Fenmore's trusts it to be good on its own, so they have drowned it. The aetheric glaze is sweet in a way that fights everything, the gold cheese is there to be looked at rather than tasted, and the lich-pepper sauce arrives with a cold numbing burn that does not build so much as loiter, long after you have stopped wanting it. It is a taco assembled by people who confused expensive with special. You are paying ten gold for three ingredients that are each performing for a different audience. Order it if you like to be dared. Do not order it if you like to eat.

## Second place, the honest one: The Grove Taco

Six gold, the cheapest on the menu, and the one I would actually recommend to a person who is hungry rather than curious. Forest-spirit greens, moonbloom cheese, whispering herb oil. It is a plain green taco and it has the confidence not to pretend otherwise. The greens are dressed and bright and taste like something that grew, the moonbloom cheese is soft and faintly floral and knows to stay quiet, and the herb oil ties it without shouting. There is nothing fire-forged about it and the menu's own poetry does it no favors, but it is the dish on this board most likely to make you feel well rather than merely impressed. In a season when half the region is chasing spectacle, there is a real argument for the six-gold taco that simply tastes good and lets you get on with your day.

## First place, and worth the walk: The Dragonfire Taco

Eight gold, and the only item on the menu that earns the whole overheated advertisement. Scorched wyvern meat, starfyre salsa, enchanted pepper relish. The wyvern is charred hard at the edges and still tender at the center, which is the entire trick of it and the hardest thing to get right, and Fenmore's gets it right. The starfyre salsa actually crackles, a bright sparking heat that arrives and then leaves like a well-mannered guest, and the pepper relish underneath gives it a sweetness with a floor to stand on. This is the taco the jingle is about. Every filling fire-forged, and for once the kitchen means it. I ordered a second one before I had finished the first, which is the only review that matters and the only one I fully trust myself to give.

## The verdict

Skip The Anomaly and its ten-gold ambitions. Get the Grove Taco if you want to eat well and cheaply and leave feeling like yourself. Get the Dragonfire Taco if you want to understand why that sponsor read will not leave any of us alone. Fenmore's is a genuinely good little establishment doing one thing superbly, one thing honestly, and one thing far too hard, which, if I am being generous, is one more good taco than most places on this island can manage.

*Topaz Toepads is the Chronicle's food critic. He pays for his own meals and, on this occasion, for two of them. Complaints, recommendations, and dares may be sent by owl.*
